Frequently Asked Questions
What is Earnings Per Share?
Earnings per share (EPS) is calculated as a company's profit divided by the outstanding shares of its common stock. The resulting number serves as an indicator of a company's profitability. Basically, higher the Earning per share, Higher the companyâ€™s profits.
What is Dividend Payout?
A company's dividend payout ratio is the percentage of that company's earnings that it pays out to its investors as dividend income. A higher payout ratio is a sign of a strong balance sheet, and we find companies with a 35% to 55% payout ratio attractive and a sign of stability.
What is Stock Price Valuation?
Stock Price Valuation basically gives an Idea to whether the Company has a Higher Valuation than it needs to have or a Lower Valuation based on the Market.
What is Return on Equity?
Return on equity (ROE) is a measure of financial performance calculated by dividing net income by shareholders' equity. ROE is considered a gauge of a corporation's profitability and how efficient it is in generating profits. Companies that post RoE of more than 15 percent are generally considered to be in a good shape.
What is Return on Capital Employed?
Return on capital employed (ROCE) is a good baseline measure of a company's performance. ROCE is a financial ratio that shows if a company is doing a good job of generating profits from its capital. companies use ROCE as a useful metric to gauge their performance. A good rule of thumb is that a ROCE of 15% or more is reflective of a decent quality business and this is almost certain to mean it is generating a good return.
